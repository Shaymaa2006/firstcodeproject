All over the world

Moscow, Russia

Released in 1991 as the lead single from Metallica's
self-titled Black Album,
"Enter Sandman" quickly became one of the
band's most iconic songs.
With its heavy riff, catchy melody,
and eerie lyrics about childhood fears,
it marked a shift toward a more polished,
mainstream sound for Metallica,
while still retaining their hard-hitting metal edge.
The song's music video,
featuring haunting visuals of a child’s
nightmares, was a staple on MTV.
At the band's 1991 Monsters of Rock festival
in Moscow, where they performed
"Enter Sandman" live, they played to an
estimated crowd of 1,600,000 people,
further solidifying the track’s legendary status in their career.

New York

The largest concert ever held in the United States in terms
of attendance was the
Rod Stewart concert at the Citi Field
in New York in 1994. This performance
drew an estimated 3.5 million
people. The concert took place on September 6,
1994, and broke the Guinness World
Record for the largest concert attendance.
The event was a part of the America's 1994
Independence Day celebration, which
coincided with the Great Lawn
of Central Park, where other events were happening that day.
While the 1994 Rod Stewart concert holds the
record for the largest audience in the U.S.,
other massive concerts have been held throughout
the years, including some featuring famous artists like
The Rolling Stones, Beyoncé, and Garth Brooks, but none
have surpassed the 3.5 million mark.

Copacabana Beach, Brazil

The Rolling Stones' 2006 concert in Brazil, held at the iconic
Maracanã Stadium in
Rio de Janeiro, was a monumental event
that captured the raw energy and
passion of both the band and their
devoted Brazilian fans. As part of their A
Bigger Bang world tour, the Stones performed
to an audience of over 1.5
million people, making it one of the largest
gatherings in the band's storied history.
The performance was a high-octane
mix of classic hits like
"Paint It Black," "Start Me Up,"
and "Sympathy for the Devil," alongside tracks
from their 2005 album A Bigger Bang. The crowd, known
for their fervent love of music, erupted in cheers, transforming
the stadium into a sea of enthusiasm.
The 2006 Brazil concert remains a testament
to The Rolling Stones' global appeal and their ability to ignite an
unforgettable atmosphere, especially in a country where rock
music has deep cultural roots.
