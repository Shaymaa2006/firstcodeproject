Moscow, Russia

Released in 1991 as the lead single from Metallica's self-titled Black Album,
"Enter Sandman" quickly became one of the band's most iconic songs.
With its heavy riff, catchy melody, and eerie lyrics about childhood fears,
it marked a shift toward a more polished, mainstream sound for Metallica,
while still retaining their hard-hitting metal edge. The song's music video,
featuring haunting visuals of a child’s nightmares, was a staple on MTV.
At the band's 1991 Monsters of Rock festival in Moscow, where they performed
"Enter Sandman" live, they played to an estimated crowd of 1,600,000 people,
further solidifying the track’s legendary status in their career.
